This Medical Device Review Toolkit provides a comprehensive framework for the pre-clinical assessment of Dental Cone-Beam CT (CBCT) systems, based on the provided regulatory guidance. It is designed for regulatory affairs specialists, manufacturers, and review personnel to ensure the safety, efficacy, and regulatory compliance of these devices.

General Principles for Pre-clinical Testing:

Scope and Applicability: This toolkit applies to Dental Cone-Beam CT (CBCT) systems, which utilize a cone-shaped X-ray beam to reconstruct three-dimensional images of the teeth, oral cavity, and maxilla/mandible regions. These systems fall under the classification of "Extraoral source x-ray system" (F.1800).
Comprehensive Validation: Manufacturers must provide complete validation and evaluation data (including pre-clinical testing and/or clinical trials) based on the product's structure, materials, and claimed performance.
Documentation Requirements: Pre-clinical test data must include test specifications (with acceptable ranges and their basis), methods, raw test records, and test reports.
Alternative Data: If specific listed tests are not performed by the manufacturer, relevant literature or scientific evaluation reports must be submitted to demonstrate equivalent safety and functionality.
Specification Deviations: If the manufacturer's specifications differ from those in this guidance or referenced standards, relevant literature or scientific evaluation reports must be provided to justify the deviation.
Test Method Deviations: If the manufacturer's test methods differ from the referenced methods:
If equivalent, the manufacturer's test method must be submitted for review.
If not equivalent, the manufacturer's test method and relevant literature or scientific evaluation reports must be submitted to justify its basis.
Standard Updates: Manufacturers may refer to updated versions of referenced standards if they have been revised, abolished, or replaced.
Dynamic Nature of Guidance: This guidance is based on current reference materials and will be updated periodically. Reviewers may request additional validation data (including pre-clinical testing and/or clinical trials) beyond the listed items, considering the product's claimed performance, structure, and design safety and functionality, to ensure public health and safety.
第一部分：臨床前審查指引 (Review Guidance)
This section outlines the key areas for pre-clinical review of Dental Cone-Beam CT systems, providing detailed considerations for each aspect.

1. 產品規格要求 (Product Specifications)
A thorough understanding and documentation of the product's specifications are fundamental for assessing its safety and performance. These specifications define the device's intended use, operational limits, and expected output.

一般物理性規格 (General Physical Specifications):

Dimensions, Volume, Weight: These parameters are crucial for installation planning, space requirements, structural integrity, and transportation logistics. They also impact the stability and safety of the device during operation, especially concerning patient positioning and operator access. Documentation should include detailed drawings and measurements.
Power Specifications: Input voltage, current, frequency, and power consumption are essential for electrical safety assessment (e.g., ensuring compatibility with hospital electrical systems, preventing overloads).
Output Power: For X-ray systems, this relates to the X-ray generator's capacity and directly influences image quality and patient dose.
Environmental Conditions: Operating and storage temperature, humidity, and atmospheric pressure ranges must be defined. These ensure the device functions reliably under specified conditions and maintains its integrity during transport and storage. Deviations can lead to performance degradation or safety hazards.
系統規格 (System Specifications):

Scanning Time: The duration of the X-ray exposure and data acquisition phase. Shorter scanning times reduce motion artifacts and patient dose, but must be balanced with image quality requirements.
Exposure Time: The actual duration the X-ray beam is active. This is a critical parameter for radiation dose calculation and control.
Rotation Angle: The angular range over which the X-ray source and detector rotate around the patient. This determines the completeness of the data acquired for 3D reconstruction and influences the Field of View (FOV).
Reconstruction Time: The time taken for the system's software to process the acquired raw data and generate the 3D volumetric image. Faster reconstruction times improve workflow efficiency.
拍攝範圍 (Field of View, FOV): The anatomical area covered by the scan. For dental CBCT, this typically includes the teeth, oral cavity, and maxilla/mandible. The FOV impacts patient dose and the diagnostic utility for specific clinical applications.
接收器 (Detector) 之規格 (Detector Specifications):

Detector Type: (e.g., flat panel detector, CCD, CMOS). The type significantly influences image quality characteristics such as resolution, noise, and dynamic range.
Working Principle: Understanding how the detector converts X-ray photons into electrical signals is essential for evaluating its performance and potential limitations.
Material: The scintillator material (e.g., Cesium Iodide) and semiconductor material (e.g., amorphous silicon) affect detection efficiency, signal-to-noise ratio, and radiation dose requirements.
感應作用區域 (Active Area): The physical size of the detector's sensitive area, which directly relates to the maximum achievable FOV.
X光管球之型號規格 (X-ray Tube Model Specifications):

焦點尺寸 (Focal Spot Size): A smaller focal spot generally leads to higher spatial resolution in the resulting images. This is a critical parameter for diagnostic clarity in dental imaging.
管電壓 (Tube Voltage, kVp): Determines the energy (penetrating power) of the X-ray beam. Higher kVp is used for denser tissues and affects image contrast.
管電流 (Tube Current, mA): Controls the quantity of X-ray photons produced per unit time. Higher mA increases image brightness and reduces noise, but also increases patient dose.
管電流時間積 (mAs): The product of tube current and exposure time, directly proportional to the total X-ray dose delivered and the number of photons reaching the detector. It's a key parameter for dose optimization and image quality.
容許誤差 (Acceptable Tolerances): Manufacturers must specify the permissible deviations for kVp, mA, and mAs, ensuring consistent and safe operation within defined limits.
影像資訊 (Image Information):

成像原理/影像重建方式 (Imaging Principle/Image Reconstruction Method): Detailed explanation of how raw data is acquired and processed into a 3D image (e.g., filtered back projection, iterative reconstruction). This impacts image quality, artifact reduction, and reconstruction time.
影像格式 (Image Format): Standard formats (e.g., DICOM) ensure interoperability with other medical imaging systems and viewing software.
演算法 (Algorithms): Description of the algorithms used for image processing, noise reduction, artifact correction, and dose optimization.
尺寸 (Dimensions): The physical dimensions of the reconstructed 3D volume.
解析度 (Resolution):
空間解析度 (Spatial Resolution): The ability to distinguish between two closely spaced objects. Critical for visualizing fine anatomical details like root canals or bone trabeculae.
對比解析度 (Contrast Resolution): The ability to distinguish between tissues with similar X-ray attenuation properties. Important for differentiating soft tissues or subtle pathological changes.
像素 (Pixel Size) / 體素 (Voxel Size): The smallest unit of information in a 2D image (pixel) or 3D volume (voxel). Smaller pixel/voxel sizes generally lead to higher spatial resolution but also larger data files.
定位系統種類及規格 (Positioning System Type and Specifications):

Description of the patient positioning mechanism (e.g., laser guides, bite blocks, headrests) and its specifications, including accuracy, reproducibility, and range of motion. Accurate patient positioning is crucial for minimizing motion artifacts and ensuring the correct anatomical region is scanned.
成像及系統其他搭配軟體名稱、版次及軟體之性能說明 (Imaging and System Software Name, Version, and Performance Description):

Identification of all software components, including image acquisition, reconstruction, viewing, and system control software.
Version numbers are critical for configuration management and traceability.
A detailed description of each software's performance, functionalities, and any specific algorithms employed. This forms the basis for software validation.
2. 生物相容性評估 (Biocompatibility)
While Dental CBCT systems are generally non-invasive, certain components may come into direct or indirect contact with the patient. Therefore, biocompatibility assessment is a critical consideration for any patient-contacting materials.

Scope of Assessment:
Identify all device components that come into direct or indirect contact with the patient (e.g., headrests, chin rests, bite blocks, patient handles, positioning aids).
Determine the nature and duration of contact (e.g., surface contact, limited duration).
Regulatory Basis: The primary standard for biocompatibility evaluation is ISO 10993 series, "Biological evaluation of medical devices."
Key Considerations:
Material Characterization: Detailed information on the chemical composition and physical properties of all patient-contacting materials.
Risk Assessment: Based on the nature and duration of patient contact, conduct a biological risk assessment to determine the necessary biocompatibility tests.
Typical Tests (if applicable): For surface-contacting devices with limited duration contact, the following tests are commonly required:
Cytotoxicity (ISO 10993-5): To assess whether the material causes cell lysis or inhibition of cell growth.
Sensitization (ISO 10993-10): To evaluate the potential for the material to induce allergic or hypersensitivity reactions.
Irritation (ISO 10993-10): To assess the potential for the material to cause local irritation to skin or mucous membranes.
Documentation: A comprehensive biocompatibility evaluation report, including material specifications, risk assessment, test protocols, raw data, and final test reports, demonstrating compliance with ISO 10993. If a material is a commonly used medical-grade material with a history of safe use, a justification based on literature review may be acceptable, but this must be robustly supported.
3. 滅菌確效 (Sterilization)
Dental CBCT systems are typically non-sterile devices. However, if any component is intended to be sterile or requires disinfection/sterilization between uses, validation of these processes is mandatory.

Scope of Assessment:
Identify any components of the CBCT system that are intended to be supplied sterile (e.g., disposable patient positioning accessories) or require sterilization/high-level disinfection by the user between patients.
If no components are intended to be sterile, a clear statement and justification should be provided.
Regulatory Basis: Relevant standards include ISO 11135 (Ethylene Oxide), ISO 11137 (Radiation), ISO 17665 (Moist Heat), and ISO 14937 (General requirements for characterization of a sterilizing agent and the development, validation and routine control of a sterilization process for medical devices).
Key Considerations (if applicable):
Sterilization Method: Clearly define the chosen sterilization method (e.g., Ethylene Oxide (EtO), gamma radiation, electron beam, steam).
Sterility Assurance Level (SAL): Typically, a SAL of 10⁻⁶ is required for sterile medical devices, meaning there is less than a one in a million chance of a non-sterile unit.
Bioburden Assessment: Determination of the microbial load on the device prior to sterilization.
Bacterial Endotoxin Testing (BET): For devices that contact blood or cerebrospinal fluid, or are labeled non-pyrogenic.
Packaging Validation (ISO 11607): Ensuring the sterile barrier system maintains sterility until the point of use and protects the device from physical damage.
Shelf-Life Studies: Demonstrating that the device remains sterile and functional for its declared shelf life.
Documentation: A comprehensive sterilization validation report, including detailed protocols, raw data, and final reports for bioburden, sterilization cycle development, routine monitoring, packaging validation, and shelf-life studies. For user-reprocessed components, detailed instructions for cleaning, disinfection, and/or sterilization, along with validation data for these processes, must be provided.
4. 機械性質評估 (Mechanical Testing)
Mechanical testing ensures the structural integrity, stability, and safe operation of the physical components of the CBCT system throughout its lifecycle, from transport and installation to routine clinical use. This is implicitly covered under the general safety and performance requirements.

Structural Integrity and Stability:
Gantry and Support Structure: Evaluation of the mechanical strength and stability of the main gantry, patient support table/chair, and X-ray tube/detector assembly. This includes static load testing (e.g., patient weight capacity) and dynamic load testing (e.g., during rotation).
Mounting and Installation: Verification that the device can be safely mounted (e.g., floor-mounted, wall-mounted) and remains stable during operation, preventing tipping or collapse.
Vibration and Shock Resistance: Testing to ensure the device can withstand vibrations and shocks encountered during transportation, installation, and normal operation without damage or performance degradation.
Moving Parts Safety:
Motorized Components: Assessment of the safety of all motorized movements, such as gantry rotation, vertical adjustment, and patient positioning. This includes testing for pinch points, crushing hazards, and emergency stop mechanisms.
Braking Systems: Verification of the effectiveness and reliability of braking mechanisms for moving parts to prevent unintended movement.
Limits of Motion: Ensuring that all movements operate within defined safe limits and that limit switches or sensors function correctly.
Durability and Lifetime:
Fatigue Testing: For critical components subject to repetitive stress (e.g., rotation mechanisms), fatigue testing may be required to demonstrate their durability over the device's expected service life.
Wear and Tear: Assessment of components prone to wear, such as bearings, cables, and connectors, to ensure they maintain functionality and safety over time.
Environmental Testing:
Temperature and Humidity: Testing the device's ability to operate safely and effectively within specified temperature and humidity ranges, and to withstand storage conditions.
Ingress Protection (IP) Rating: If applicable, testing the device's protection against ingress of liquids or solid foreign objects, particularly for components that may be exposed to spills or dust.
Regulatory Basis:
IEC 60601-1 (General Requirements for Basic Safety and Essential Performance): This standard covers a wide range of mechanical hazards, including stability, moving parts, forces, and surfaces.
IEC 60601-2-63 (Particular Requirements for Dental Extra-oral X-ray Equipment): This particular standard provides specific requirements for the mechanical safety of dental X-ray equipment.
Documentation: Detailed mechanical test reports, including test protocols, acceptance criteria, raw data, and results for all relevant mechanical assessments. This may include structural analysis, stress calculations, and environmental test reports.
5. 特定風險與額外評估 (Special Risks and Additional Evaluations)
This section addresses critical safety and performance aspects specific to X-ray emitting medical devices and complex systems, along with other essential regulatory considerations.

5.1. 電性安全試驗 (Electrical Safety Test)
Electrical safety is paramount for medical electrical equipment to protect patients, operators, and the surrounding environment from electrical hazards.

Objective: To ensure the product does not pose a hazard (e.g., electrical shock, fire, excessive temperature) during normal operation, single fault conditions, transport, storage, installation, or maintenance.
Key Aspects:
Protection against Electric Shock: Insulation, protective earthing, leakage currents, dielectric strength.
Mechanical Hazards: Stability, moving parts, forces, and surfaces that could cause injury.
Protection against Unwanted and Excessive Radiation: Specific to X-ray devices, ensuring radiation leakage is within limits.
Protection against Excessive Temperatures and Other Hazards: Fire hazards, explosion hazards (if applicable), and other potential risks.
Essential Performance: Verification that the device maintains its essential performance under specified conditions.
Regulatory Basis:
IEC 60601-1:2005: Medical Electrical Equipment - Part 1: General Requirements for Basic Safety and Essential Performance.
IEC 60601-2-63:2012: Medical electrical equipment - Part 2-63: Particular requirements for the basic safety and essential performance of dental extra-oral X-ray equipment.
Documentation: Comprehensive electrical safety test report demonstrating compliance with the referenced IEC standards.
5.2. 電磁相容性試驗 (Electromagnetic Compatibility Test)
Medical devices must function correctly in their electromagnetic environment without causing or being susceptible to electromagnetic interference.

Objective: To ensure the product does not emit electromagnetic interference that could affect the basic performance of other radio equipment or instruments, and that it is immune to external electromagnetic interference without deviating from its basic performance.
Key Aspects:
Emissions: Measurement of conducted and radiated electromagnetic emissions to ensure they are below specified limits.
Immunity: Testing the device's ability to withstand various electromagnetic phenomena (e.g., electrostatic discharge, radiated RF fields, electrical fast transients, surge, conducted disturbances, power frequency magnetic fields, voltage dips/interruptions) without degradation of essential performance.
Regulatory Basis:
IEC 60601-1-2:2005: Medical Electrical Equipment - Part 1-2: General Requirements for Basic Safety; Electromagnetic Compatibility - Requirements and Tests.
Documentation: EMC test report detailing emissions and immunity test results.
5.3. 輻射安全 (Radiation Safety)
For X-ray emitting devices, radiation safety is a paramount concern, requiring stringent controls to protect patients, operators, and bystanders.

Objective: To ensure that radiation exposure doses for patients, equipment operators, and surrounding personnel are kept within reasonable limits, adhering to the ALARA (As Low As Reasonably Achievable) principle.
Key Aspects:
Dose Optimization: Strategies and features implemented to minimize patient radiation dose while maintaining diagnostic image quality.
Shielding: Design and verification of shielding to limit stray radiation and leakage from the X-ray tube housing.
Collimation: Verification of the X-ray beam collimation system to ensure the beam is restricted to the area of interest, minimizing unnecessary exposure.
Radiation Output Accuracy and Reproducibility: Regular calibration and testing to ensure the X-ray output (kVp, mA, mAs) is accurate and reproducible.
Patient and Operator Protection: Features such as lead aprons, protective barriers, and interlocks.
Quality Assurance of Radiation Output: Procedures for routine checks and maintenance to ensure consistent radiation safety.
Regulatory Basis:
IEC 60601-1-3:2008: Medical electrical equipment - Part 1-3: General requirements for basic safety and essential performance - Collateral Standard: Radiation protection in diagnostic X-ray equipment.
IEC 60601-2-63:2012: Medical electrical equipment - Part 2-63: Particular requirements for the basic safety and essential performance of dental extra-oral X-ray equipment.
Atomic Energy Council (AEC) Regulations (Taiwan): Compliance with local radiation safety regulations is mandatory. Manufacturers must refer to the AEC website for specific requirements.
Documentation: Radiation safety review data, including dose reports, shielding calculations, quality assurance protocols, and compliance certificates from relevant authorities.
5.4. 功能性試驗 (Performance Test)
Functional performance testing verifies that the CBCT system performs as intended and meets its claimed specifications, delivering diagnostically useful images.

X光管規格驗證 (X-ray Tube Specifications Verification):
Verification of actual focal spot size, tube voltage (kVp), tube current (mA), and tube current-time product (mAs) against specified values and tolerances. This ensures consistent and accurate X-ray generation.
影像規格驗證 (Image Specifications Verification):
影像尺寸 (Image Dimensions): Verification of the reconstructed image volume dimensions.
解析度 (Resolution):
空間解析度 (Spatial Resolution): Measured using line pair phantoms or modulation transfer function (MTF) analysis.
對比解析度 (Contrast Resolution): Measured using low-contrast phantoms to assess the ability to differentiate subtle density differences.
影像雜訊 (Image Noise): Quantification of random fluctuations in image signal, which can obscure fine details.
均勻度 (Uniformity): Assessment of image consistency across the entire field of view.
準確度 (Accuracy): Geometric accuracy (e.g., distance measurements) and densitometric accuracy (e.g., CT value linearity).
CT值 (CT Value): Verification of the Hounsfield Unit (HU) values for known materials (e.g., water, air, bone-equivalent materials) to ensure quantitative accuracy.
影像品質評估 (Image Quality Assessment):
訊號雜訊比 (Signal-to-Noise Ratio, SNR): A measure of image quality, indicating the strength of the signal relative to background noise.
對比訊號比 (Contrast-to-Noise Ratio, CNR): Measures the ability to distinguish an object from its background, considering both contrast and noise.
失真度 (Distortion):
DQE (Detective Quantum Efficiency): A measure of the detector's efficiency in converting X-ray photons into a useful image signal.
MTF (Modulation Transfer Function): Quantifies the system's ability to transfer contrast from the object to the image at different spatial frequencies.
影像失真 (Aliasing): Assessment of artifacts caused by undersampling.
假影 (Artifacts): Evaluation and characterization of common artifacts (e.g., metal artifacts, motion artifacts, beam hardening artifacts) and the system's ability to mitigate them.
操作參數驗證 (Operational Parameters Verification):
Verification of scanning time, exposure time, rotation angle, and Field of View (FOV) against specified values.
2D轉3D成像功能 (2D to 3D Imaging Functionality):
If the system includes functionality to convert 2D images into 3D reconstructions, evaluation of image reconstruction time and the accuracy of the resulting 3D volumetric image.
定位系統之準確度評估報告 (Positioning System Accuracy Evaluation Report):
Verification of the accuracy and reproducibility of the patient positioning system.
軟體確效 (Software Validation):
Comprehensive validation of all software components, including imaging acquisition software, image reconstruction software, image viewing software, and any other system control software. This ensures the software performs as intended, is free from critical bugs, and meets regulatory requirements.
Regulatory Basis:
IEC 60601-1:2005: General safety and essential performance.
IEC 60601-2-63:2012: Specific requirements for dental extra-oral X-ray equipment.
FDA Guidance: Relevant FDA guidance documents (e.g., for X-ray imaging devices, solid-state X-ray imaging devices) provide valuable insights into performance testing expectations.
醫療器材軟體確效指引 (2017) (Medical Device Software Validation Guidance): Local guidance for software validation.
IEC 62304:2006/Amd 1:2015: Medical device software – Software life cycle processes.
Documentation: Detailed performance test reports for all listed items, including test protocols, acceptance criteria, raw data, and results. A comprehensive software validation report is also required.
5.5. 風險管理 (Risk Management)
Although not explicitly listed as a test item in the provided text, risk management is a fundamental and mandatory regulatory requirement for all medical devices.

Objective: To identify, analyze, evaluate, control, and monitor risks associated with the medical device throughout its entire lifecycle, ensuring that residual risks are acceptable.
Key Aspects:
Risk Management Plan: A documented plan outlining the scope, responsibilities, and activities for risk management.
Risk Analysis: Identification of hazards and estimation of associated risks (severity and probability).
Risk Evaluation: Decision-making process to determine if risks are acceptable.
Risk Control: Implementation of measures to reduce risks to an acceptable level (e.g., design changes, protective measures, information for safety).
Evaluation of Overall Residual Risk: Assessment of the remaining risks after control measures are implemented.
Post-Market Surveillance: Monitoring the device in the market for new risks or changes in existing risks.
Regulatory Basis: ISO 14971:2019 (Medical devices – Application of risk management to medical devices) is the internationally recognized standard.
Documentation: A comprehensive Risk Management File, including the Risk Management Plan, Risk Analysis Report, Risk Evaluation Report, Risk Control Measures, and Post-Market Surveillance Plan.
5.6. 臨床評估 (Clinical Evaluation)
While the guidance emphasizes pre-clinical testing, it also states that manufacturers should provide complete validation data, including clinical trials if necessary, based on the product's structure, materials, and claimed performance.

Objective: To demonstrate the clinical safety and performance of the device.
Key Aspects:
Equivalence to Predicate Devices: If the CBCT system is substantially equivalent to an existing legally marketed device, a robust clinical evaluation report based on literature review and comparison may suffice.
Novelty or Significant Differences: If the device is novel, has a new intended use, or significant technological differences from predicate devices, clinical data from human studies (clinical trials) may be required.
Regulatory Basis: ISO 14155 (Clinical investigation of medical devices for human subjects – Good clinical practice) for clinical trials, and MEDDEV 2.7/1 Rev. 4 (Guidelines on Medical Devices: Clinical Evaluation) for clinical evaluation reports.
Documentation: Clinical Evaluation Report (CER) or Clinical Investigation Report (CIR) if clinical trials were conducted.
