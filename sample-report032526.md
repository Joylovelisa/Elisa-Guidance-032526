Medical Device Review Toolkit: uMI Panvivo PET/CT System
第一部分：臨床前審查指引 (Review Guidance)
This section outlines the key areas for preclinical review, providing detailed considerations and expectations for documentation and testing.

1. 產品規格要求 (Product Specifications)
The review of product specifications is fundamental to understanding the device's design, intended function, and performance capabilities. It ensures that the device meets its stated claims and is suitable for its intended use.

1.1. 產品名稱與型號 (Product Name and Models)

Guidance: Verify that all proposed device names and models are clearly identified and consistently used throughout the submission. Any variations or suffixes (e.g., S, ES, EX) must be explained, detailing the specific differences between each model.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: The submission identifies uMI Panvivo, uMI Panvivo S, uMI Panvivo ES, and uMI Panvivo EX. The primary modification for ES and EX models involves scaling to 180 and 240 PET rings, respectively, compared to the 100 and 80 PET rings of the base uMI Panvivo and uMI Panvivo S. These changes directly impact performance specifications like sensitivity.
1.2. 預期用途與適應症 (Intended Use and Indications for Use)

Guidance: The intended use describes the general purpose of the device, while indications for use specify the diseases or conditions the device will diagnose, treat, or prevent, and the target patient population. Both must be clear, precise, and supported by the device's design and performance data. Any limitations or specific conditions for use must be explicitly stated.
uMI Panvivo Specifics:
Intended Use: A PET/CT system for providing anatomical and functional images, assessing metabolic (molecular) and physiologic functions in various parts of the body (whole body, brain, head and neck, heart, lung, breast, gastrointestinal, urinary system and genital organ, musculoskeletal systems, and others).
Indications for Use: Images aid in detection, localization, evaluation, diagnosis, staging, re-staging, monitoring, and/or follow-up of abnormalities, lesions, tumors, inflammation, infection, organ function, disorders, and/or diseases in clinical areas like oncology, cardiology, neurology, infection, and inflammation. Also for radiotherapy treatment planning and interventional radiology procedures.
Specific Condition: The CT system can be used for low-dose CT lung cancer screening for early detection of lung nodules, provided the screening is performed within established inclusion criteria of approved programs/protocols by a governmental body or professional medical society. This requires reference to clinical literature (e.g., National Lung Screening Trial).
Radiopharmaceuticals: The system must be used with radiopharmaceuticals approved by the regulatory authority in the country of use.
Review Focus: Ensure consistency between the intended use and indications. Verify that the clinical areas and applications are appropriate for a PET/CT system. Confirm that the lung cancer screening caveat is clearly communicated in labeling.
1.3. 裝置描述與組成 (Device Description and Components)

Guidance: A detailed description of the device's physical characteristics, operational principles, and all components is essential. This includes hardware, software, and accessories. Any modifications from a predicate device must be highlighted and justified.
uMI Panvivo Specifics:
Core System: Combines a PET system (295/235/534/712 mm axial FOV) and a 160-slice CT system.
Components: PET system, CT system, patient table, power distribution unit, control and reconstruction system (host, monitor, reconstruction computer, system software, reconstruction software), vital signal module, and other accessories.
Modifications:
New Models: uMI Panvivo ES (180 PET rings) and uMI Panvivo EX (240 PET rings) added, increasing native sensitivity.
Patient Table Load: Increased to 280 kg, validated by system tests, with no design changes.
Software Features:
NeuroFocus.Brain: Brain-artifact elimination using statistics-based motion detection.
uExcel DPR: Algorithmically identical to previous versions, but ES/EX models only support FDG (¹⁸F-FDG), while base models support several imaging agents.
Intelligent Assistant: Local auxiliary query tool for PET/CT operations, based on validated knowledge, without controlling or modifying system hardware/software/parameters.
DeepMAC: Image post-processing using pre-trained neural networks to reduce metal artifacts.
Review Focus: Assess the impact of increased PET rings on performance and safety. Verify the justification for the increased patient table load. Evaluate the new software features for their functionality, validation, and potential impact on safety and effectiveness.
1.4. 性能規格 (Performance Specifications)

Guidance: Quantitative and qualitative performance specifications must be provided, demonstrating the device's operational capabilities. This includes imaging parameters, resolution, sensitivity, and other relevant metrics.
uMI Panvivo Specifics:
Sensitivity: Proposed devices have larger sensitivities than predicate devices, leading to better image quality.
NECR Peak Value & Peak True Count Rate: Proposed devices provide larger values, allowing for more effective data acquisition even at high activity concentrations.
Image Performance: Verified through non-clinical testing to meet design specifications and demonstrate substantial equivalence.
Clinical Image Evaluation: Sample clinical images reviewed by U.S. board-certified radiologists, confirming diagnostic sufficiency.
Review Focus: Compare performance data against established benchmarks (e.g., NEMA NU 2-2018) and the predicate device. Ensure that improved performance metrics do not introduce new safety concerns.
2. 生物相容性評估 (Biocompatibility)
Biocompatibility evaluation assesses the biological response of a patient to the device materials. For a PET/CT system, direct patient contact is primarily with the patient table and potentially vital signal module components or accessories.

Guidance: Identify all patient-contacting materials and components. Classify the nature and duration of patient contact according to ISO 10993-1. Conduct appropriate biological tests based on this classification.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: The submission claims conformance to ISO 10993-1:2018 (Biological evaluation of medical devices - Part 1: Evaluation and testing within a risk management process), ISO 10993-5:2009 (Tests for in vitro cytotoxicity), and ISO 10993-10:2010 (Tests for irritation and skin sensitization).
Review Focus:
Identification of Contacting Parts: Clearly list all parts of the uMI Panvivo system that come into direct or indirect contact with the patient (e.g., patient table surface, vital signal module sensors, patient positioning aids, accessory cushions).
Contact Type and Duration: Classify the contact (e.g., surface contact, intact skin) and duration (e.g., limited <24h, prolonged 24h-30 days). For a PET/CT system, contact is typically surface and prolonged.
Test Reports: Review detailed test reports for cytotoxicity, irritation, and sensitization for all relevant materials. Ensure tests were conducted according to the cited ISO 10993 standards and Good Laboratory Practices (GLP).
Risk Assessment: Confirm that the biological risk assessment, as part of the overall risk management process (ISO 14971), adequately addresses potential material-related risks.
Material Information: Request detailed material composition for patient-contacting parts, including any colorants, additives, or processing aids.
3. 滅菌確效 (Sterilization)
For large capital equipment like a PET/CT system, terminal sterilization of the entire device is generally not applicable. However, specific components or accessories that are intended to be sterile or require high-level disinfection must be addressed.

Guidance: If any component of the device is supplied sterile or requires sterilization/high-level disinfection between uses, robust validation data must be provided. This includes method selection, cycle development, routine monitoring, and maintenance of sterility assurance. For non-sterile patient-contacting surfaces, detailed cleaning and disinfection protocols are required.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: The provided text does not explicitly mention sterilization. Given the nature of a PET/CT system, it is primarily a non-sterile device. However, patient-contacting surfaces and certain accessories require rigorous infection control.
Review Focus:
Infection Control Protocols: Evaluate the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning, disinfection, and maintenance of patient-contacting surfaces (e.g., patient table, vital signal module components). Ensure these protocols are clear, validated, and effective against relevant microorganisms.
Material Compatibility: Verify that the materials used for patient-contacting parts are compatible with the recommended cleaning and disinfection agents, ensuring no degradation over the device's lifespan.
Accessories: If any accessories are intended for single-use or require sterilization, demand full sterilization validation reports (e.g., ISO 11135 for EO, ISO 11137 for radiation, ISO 17665 for moist heat).
Risk Assessment: Confirm that infection control risks are adequately addressed in the device's risk management file.
4. 機械性質評估 (Mechanical Testing)
Mechanical testing ensures the structural integrity, durability, and safe operation of the device, particularly for components subject to physical stress, movement, or patient load.

Guidance: All critical mechanical components, especially those supporting patient weight or undergoing movement, must be thoroughly tested to demonstrate their safety and reliability under normal and foreseeable abnormal conditions.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: The patient table's maximum load specification was increased to 280 kg, confirmed by system tests. This revision was justified by an excessive safety margin in original specifications, with no changes to the bed's design.
Review Focus:
Patient Table Load Capacity: Review the detailed test reports for the patient table, verifying that the 280 kg load capacity is robustly supported. This should include static load, dynamic load (e.g., during table movement), and stability tests.
Structural Integrity: Evaluate the mechanical design and testing of other critical structural components, such as the gantry, scanning mechanisms, and any moving parts. This includes:
Fatigue Testing: To ensure components can withstand repeated cycles of use over the device's expected lifespan.
Stress Analysis: To confirm that materials and designs can handle maximum operational stresses without failure.
Vibration and Shock Testing: To ensure the device can withstand environmental stresses during transport and operation.
Safety Mechanisms: Review the design and testing of any mechanical safety features, such as emergency stops, anti-collision systems, and patient fall prevention mechanisms.
Material Properties: Verify that the mechanical properties of materials used in critical components (e.g., tensile strength, yield strength, hardness) are appropriate for their intended function.
Risk Assessment: Ensure that mechanical failures and their potential harm to patients or operators are identified and mitigated within the risk management file.
5. 特定風險與額外評估 (Special Risks and Additional Evaluations)
This section covers specialized evaluations pertinent to the PET/CT system, including electrical safety, electromagnetic compatibility, radiation safety, software validation, cybersecurity, usability, and risk management.

5.1. 電氣安全與電磁相容性 (Electrical Safety and Electromagnetic Compatibility - EMC)

Guidance: Medical electrical equipment must comply with stringent standards to ensure patient and operator safety from electrical hazards and to prevent electromagnetic interference with other devices.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: Conformance to ANSI/AAMI ES60601-1:2005/(R)2012+A1:2012+C1:2009/(R)2012+A2:2010/(R)2012)[IncludingAmendment2(2021)] (General requirements for basic safety and essential performance), IEC 60601-1-2:2014+A1:2020 (EMC), and IEC TR 60601-4-2:2016 (EMC guidance).
Review Focus:
Test Reports: Review comprehensive test reports demonstrating compliance with all clauses of the cited standards, including insulation, leakage currents, protective earthing, mechanical strength, and environmental conditions.
Risk Management: Verify that electrical and EMC risks are identified, analyzed, and mitigated in the risk management file.
Labeling: Ensure appropriate electrical safety warnings and EMC information are included in the user manual and device labeling.
5.2. 輻射安全 (Radiation Safety)

Guidance: As a device emitting ionizing radiation (X-rays for CT, positrons for PET), rigorous radiation safety measures are paramount to protect patients, operators, and the public.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: Conformance to IEC 60601-1-3:2008+AMD1:2013+A2:2021 (Radiation protection in diagnostic X-ray equipment), IEC 60601-2-44:2009+A1:2012+A2:2016 (Particular requirements for X-ray equipment for computed tomography), and NEMA NU 2-2018 (Performance Measurements of Positron Emission Tomographs). Compliance with Code of Federal Regulations, Title 21, Subchapter J - Radiological Health.
Review Focus:
Dose Optimization: Evaluate mechanisms for dose optimization (e.g., automatic exposure control for CT, low-dose protocols).
Image Quality vs. Dose: Assess the balance between diagnostic image quality and radiation dose, especially for lung cancer screening.
Shielding and Leakage: Review data on radiation shielding and leakage radiation measurements.
Performance Measurements: Verify PET performance metrics (e.g., spatial resolution, sensitivity, scatter fraction, count rate performance) against NEMA NU 2-2018.
Quality Assurance: Review the proposed QA/QC procedures for radiation output and image quality.
Operator Training: Ensure adequate training requirements for operators regarding radiation safety.
Labeling: Verify that radiation safety warnings, dose information, and operating instructions are clear and prominent.
5.3. 軟體確效與網路安全 (Software Validation and Cybersecurity)

Guidance: Software is a critical component of modern medical devices. Its development, validation, and maintenance must follow a robust lifecycle process. Cybersecurity is essential to protect patient data and device functionality from unauthorized access or manipulation.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: Conformance to IEC 62304:2006+AMD1:2015 CSV (Medical device software - Software life cycle processes), "Guidance for the Content of Premarket Submissions for Software Contained in Medical Devices," and "Content of Premarket Submissions for Management of Cybersecurity in Medical Devices." DICOM conformance (NEMA PS 3.1-3.20). New software features: NeuroFocus.Brain, uExcel DPR, Intelligent Assistant, DeepMAC.
Review Focus:
Software Life Cycle: Review the software development plan, requirements, design, implementation, verification, and validation documentation according to IEC 62304.
Risk Management: Assess software-related risks, including potential failures, errors, and their impact on patient safety and device effectiveness.
Cybersecurity:
Threat Modeling: Review the cybersecurity risk assessment, including threat modeling and vulnerability analysis.
Controls: Evaluate implemented cybersecurity controls (e.g., access control, data encryption, patch management, incident response plan).
Documentation: Verify adherence to FDA cybersecurity guidance, including a cybersecurity bill of materials (CBOM).
DICOM Conformance: Confirm the device's ability to communicate and exchange images and data according to DICOM standards.
New Software Features Validation:
NeuroFocus.Brain: Validate the motion detection algorithm and its impact on image quality and diagnostic accuracy.
uExcel DPR: Verify the algorithm's performance and the specific radiopharmaceutical support for each model.
Intelligent Assistant: Confirm it is purely an auxiliary tool and does not control or modify core system functions. Validate the knowledge base.
DeepMAC (AI/ML): This requires specific attention.
AI/ML Validation: Review the validation strategy for DeepMAC, including:
Training Data: Source, diversity, representativeness, and annotation quality of the system simulations used for training.
Validation Datasets: Details of PMMA phantom datasets and clinical datasets (20 human subjects, diverse demographics) used for validation.
Performance Metrics: How the reduction of metal artifacts and improvement in image quality were quantitatively and qualitatively assessed.
Bias and Robustness: Evaluation for potential biases and robustness across different patient populations, metal types, and scan conditions.
Transparency: Understanding the algorithm's decision-making process where possible.
Risk Management for AI/ML: Address specific risks associated with AI/ML, such as algorithmic bias, unintended consequences, and model drift.
5.4. 可用性評估 (Usability Evaluation)

Guidance: Usability engineering ensures that the device is safe and effective for its intended users in its intended use environment, minimizing the risk of use errors.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: Conformance to IEC 60601-1-6:2010+A1:2013+A2:2020 (Usability).
Review Focus:
Usability Engineering File: Review the usability engineering process, including user and use environment analysis, hazard analysis related to use error, and formative and summative usability testing.
User Interface: Evaluate the user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) for clarity, intuitiveness, and error prevention.
Instructions for Use (IFU): Ensure the IFU is clear, comprehensive, and easy to understand, providing all necessary information for safe and effective operation.
5.5. 雷射安全 (Laser Safety)

Guidance: If the device incorporates lasers (e.g., for patient positioning), it must comply with laser safety standards to prevent eye or skin injury.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: Conformance to IEC 60825-1:2014 (Safety of laser products - Part 1: Equipment classification and requirements).
Review Focus:
Laser Classification: Verify the laser class and ensure appropriate controls are in place for that class.
Safety Features: Review interlocks, warning labels, and protective housing.
Test Reports: Demand test reports confirming laser output power and wavelength are within safe limits.
5.6. 風險管理 (Risk Management)

Guidance: A comprehensive risk management process is essential for identifying, evaluating, controlling, and monitoring risks throughout the device's lifecycle.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: Conformance to ISO 14971:2019 (Medical Devices – Application of risk management to medical devices).
Review Focus:
Risk Management File: Review the complete risk management file, including the risk management plan, risk analysis, risk evaluation, risk control measures, and evaluation of overall residual risk.
Traceability: Ensure traceability between identified hazards, harms, risk control measures, and verification activities.
Post-Market Surveillance: Verify that the risk management plan includes provisions for post-market surveillance and review of new risk information.
5.7. 品質系統 (Quality System)

Guidance: The manufacturer must operate under a robust quality management system to ensure consistent product quality and regulatory compliance.
uMI Panvivo Specifics: Conformance to Code of Federal Regulations, Title 21, Part 820 - Quality System Regulation.
Review Focus:
QMS Documentation: Verify the existence and implementation of a comprehensive Quality Management System (QMS) compliant with 21 CFR 820.
Design Controls: Ensure design controls are adequately applied throughout the device development process.
Manufacturing Controls: Review manufacturing process controls, including process validation and acceptance criteria.
